Wandering Minds is a social project dedicated to raising awareness about mental/psychological disorders and to #BreaktheStigma . We aspire to change the social phobia and misunderstanding of those conflicted by their own personal psychological disabilities. We are aiming to personify the unidentified - to convey a clear understanding of the mentally afflicted - to clear a path that has been littered with skepticism and misunderstanding to allow the many who suffer the conventions of their minds. To let them wander among the possibilities of social and professional aid.

There is a silent stigma that is overtaking our social psyche, one that is so prevalent that nearly 10 million people are suffering with a mental illness, yet less than an estimated 50% are receiving the treatment they need. Wandering Minds is a campaigner for mental disorder/disease awareness and we are pushing against the norm to #BreaktheStigma. Suicide is the 3rd leading cause of death for 15-24 year olds, 90% of which suffer from a mental disorder. This stigma is especially true of those from African American, Asian and Hispanic backgrounds. Awareness of the mental health of others and ourselves has become a necessity!

What is NAMI ?

The National Alliance On Mental Illness (NAMI) is a nationwide grassroots advocacy group, representing families and people affected by mental illness in the United States. NAMI provides psychoeducation, research and support for people and their families impacted by mental illness through various public education and awareness activities. Headquartered in Arlington, Virginia, NAMI has state and local affiliates, all operating mainly with the work of thousands of volunteers. Members of NAMI are typically consumers of mental health services, family members, and professionals working together toward a common goal.
